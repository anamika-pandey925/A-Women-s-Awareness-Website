WHAT IS SURAKSHA ?

SURAKSHA is a platform dedicated to women's safety and empowerment, aimed at creating a fear-free society. This platform offers the best educational support for women in both urban and rural areas, addressing the critical issue of women's safety due to increasing violence, particularly in urban settings.

Ensuring women's safety in smart cities is essential, yet despite technological advancements, it continues to be a significant global challenge. Although law enforcement keeps criminal records, they are not readily accessible.

Efforts and Challenges in Addressing Women’s Safety in India

Over the past seven years, India has witnessed significant efforts to address women’s safety. However, challenges persist due to socio-economic, cultural, and geographical factors.

Despite new laws, initiatives, and technological interventions, women in both urban and rural areas continue to face threats of harassment, domestic violence, and assault. The social fabric remains vulnerable, and more work is needed to address these persistent issues.

Women Safety & Crime Statistics

Key Statistics on Crimes Against Women (2017-2023)

  • A consistent rise in crimes against women was reported in both rural and urban areas.
  • The National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) reports a disturbing trend of rising crimes against women in India. In 2022, there were 445,256 cases reported, representing an increase of over 31% from 2014. Of these, 7.1% were rape cases.
  • Data Source: National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB), Rajasthan Police Reports.

Urban vs. Rural Women’s Safety

  • Urban areas report higher cases of workplace harassment and cybercrime, while rural regions face more domestic violence and human trafficking.
  • Case Study: Udaipur (urban) – high-profile incidents of workplace harassment in tech firms.
  • Case Study: Barmer (rural) – alarming rates of domestic violence and honor killings due to socio-cultural pressures.

Challenges in Implementing Women’s Safety Programs

  • Lack of awareness and digital literacy in rural areas hinders the effective use of safety apps.
  • Insufficient police resources and delayed response times, particularly in remote regions.
  • Cultural stigma still prevents women from reporting crimes, particularly sexual violence.
